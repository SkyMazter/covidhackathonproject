COVID-19 Timeline

Coronavirus Timeline

Late 2019 - first detected in Wuhan, China. Cases mostly link to China's wet wholesale market through animal to human, possibly a bat or pangolins.

Jaunaury 7, 2020 - China identifies the disease as a new type of coronavirus.

January 11, 2020 - China reports it's first death, a 61 year old man who was a frequent customer of the Wholesale market.

January 13, 2020 - First reported case outside of China, in Thailand.

January 19, 2020 - First Coronavirus case in the U.S. a 35-year-old man in Washington state.

January 23, 2020 - China government impose lockdown on Wuhan.

January 31, 2020 - President Trump bans international travel of foreigners from China for two weeks.

Febuary 2, 2020 - First death from the virus outside of China was reported in the Philippines.

Febuary 11, 2020 - The disease is officially called "COVID-19" by World Health Organization.

Febuary 12-19 - Cases found in South Korea and Iran.

Febuary 21 - The outbreak in Italy starts.

Febuary 29 - The first death of coronavirus case in the U.S.

March 8 - Italy starts lockdown on 60 million infected.

March 11 - officially called pandemic.

March 11 - Trump bans all travel from Europe.

March 13 - U.S. government declares national emergency.

March 23 - New York becomes the epicenter of the Coronavirus in the U.S., with 21,000 reported cases.

March 28 - Kansas becomes the first American state to go into lockdown.
